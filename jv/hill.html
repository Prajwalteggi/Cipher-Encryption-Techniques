Cipher Knowledge

How the Vigenère Cipher Works

The Vigenère cipher uses a keyword to shift letters in the plaintext. Each letter in the keyword determines how much to shift the corresponding plaintext letter.

Encryption: C = (P + K) mod 26
Decryption: P = (C - K) mod 26

Historical Significance

Invented by Giovan Battista Bellaso in 1553, later misattributed to Blaise de Vigenère. It was considered unbreakable for centuries until Friedrich Kasiski published a successful attack in 1863.

Security Features

The cipher uses polyalphabetic substitution, meaning each plaintext letter can be encrypted to different ciphertext letters depending on its position. This makes frequency analysis much more difficult.

Key Principles

The strength of the cipher depends on the key length. Longer keys provide better security. The key is repeated to match the length of the plaintext.
